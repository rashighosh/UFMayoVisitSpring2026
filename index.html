"The Living Grant"

Iterative Prototype Relay
⭐ Group Activity

Teams build a physical or paper prototype of a digital health app feature for a specific user. Midway through, people rotate teams — simulating staff turnover — and have 90 seconds to get briefed by whoever stayed. Then a budget cut forces each team to physically remove or cross out two components and decide what survives. Teams pitch their final (bruised) prototype and the room votes on what they'd actually want as a patient.

0–2 min
Setup. Split into 3 mixed UF/Mayo groups (~6 people each). Distribute user need cards, paper, markers, post-its, tape — whatever's on the table.
2–10 min
Round 1 — Build it. Each team builds a physical/paper prototype for their user need. Rule: every person must contribute at least one component. Example user needs: "Maria, 67, just diagnosed, overwhelmed — needs help understanding what a clinical trial is" / "James, 45, two jobs, no time — needs to stay enrolled without it taking over his life" / "Priya, study coordinator managing 80 participants — needs to know who's falling through the cracks."
10–12 min
The Handoff. Facilitator announces: "Two of your team members just got new jobs. Congratulations to them." Rotate 2–3 people clockwise. The new arrivals get 90 seconds of briefing from whoever stayed.
12–18 min
Round 2 — Iterate. Teams continue building with mixed old/new members. New user need wrinkle introduced.
~15 min mark
💥 The Budget Cut. Facilitator interrupts: "Bad news — funding got cut by 40%. Physically remove or cross out 2 components. What do you cut and why?" The new people who just joined will disagree with the veterans. That tension is the point.
18–26 min
Pitches. Each team does a 90-second walkthrough — including what got cut and why. Room votes on which feature they'd actually want as a patient.
26–29 min
Debrief. Two questions: "What survived every round — and why?" and "What got lost in translation, and whose fault was that?"
Takeaways What happens to a vision when people leave, new people arrive, and resources shrink — and how do you protect what matters most. Extremely relatable for a multi-site grant team that's been through exactly this.
⏱ Total: ~30 minutes · Materials: paper, markers, post-its, tape (whatever's in the room)

💻 VERG Tech Session

Hands-on activity to demonstrate how agentic AI workflows can compliment our existing workflows/processes. Led by tech team.

🏗️

Working with Agentic AI

5 min intro → 20 min build → 5 min share-out

Split into 2 mixed groups (UF + Mayo in each). Each group goes to a station led by a tech person. Everyone participates in building something live. At the end, each group does a 90-second demo. Room votes on the most useful workflow.

The Big Idea Traditional software does what you tell it to. Agentic AI figures out what needs to happen, breaks it into steps, calls the right tools, and hands you a result. Each group demonstrates a different agentic pattern.
🟢 Group 1 · Claude

UI/Feature Mockups — Quickly iteration of interface ideas

Current challenge: We currently spend hours in Figma for rough UI mockups.

Goal: Turn plain text notes / feature requests from meetings into 2–3 quick UI mock‑ups using an AI agent.

Task: Grab 1-2 real feature request notes → Craft a prompt that asks the AI to generate multiple UI mock‑ups (e.g., low‑fidelity layouts, component ideas). → Note: structural constraints (mobile vs web, key sections, tone).

Agentic concept: Using no-code AI tools to quickly turn ideas into multiple visual pathways.

🔧 Claude
🟡 Group 2 · NotebookLM

Quarterly Reports - Quickly generate tech dev updates

Current challenge: Every quarter, tech dev re‑assembles Google Doc + GitHub info manually for report updates.

Goal: Use an agentic AI (e.g., NotebookLM reading our docs + GitHub) to auto‑generate structured quarterly‑style reports.

Task: Pick one sample quarter’s worth of data (commits, issues, Google Doc text) → Craft a prompt to fill out a template for tech dev updates (new features, maintenance, progress, blockers, next steps)

Agentic concept: Using RAG (retrieval augmented generation) to pull from relevant sources and generate relevant information

🔧 NotebookLM
Share-Out + Takeaways Each group shares/presents what they built, hopefully demonstrating how we can use agentic AI to save time + iterate faster + address manual processes!
